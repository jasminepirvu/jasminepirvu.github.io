"I had a plantation in Africa, at the foot of the Ngong Mountains"

I had a plantation in Africa, at the foot of the Ngong Mountains Thus begins the story of the plantation in Kenya where Karen Blixen lived and worked in the last years of European colonialism. Coffee is grown on the plantation. The narrator of the story, the first person, is a Danish woman, Baroness Karen Blixen. She emigrated to Kenya in 1914 with her husband Bror von Blixen - Finecke. Her best friend in Africa is Denys Finch - Hatton...

The coffee crops fail and debts mount. She is forced to sell the plantation and will go to Denmark have to return. Just after the plantation is sold, Karen receives word that Denys has died at aplane crash. His plane crashed near Nairobi. She chooses a place to bury him: in the Ngong Mountains, in a place where you can look out over the low plains. She later hears that there are regulartwo lions come and sit on the grave of Denys.

"If I know a song of Africa - I mused - of the giraffe and of the African new moon, of the plows in the field and the sweaty faces of the coffee pickers, does Africa know a song of mine?"

"Out of Africa: based on a true story"

Out of Africa was made into a 1985 film by director Sydney Pollack starring Meryl Streep (Karen Blixen), Robert Redford (Denys Finch-Hatton) and Klaus Maria Brandauer (Baron Blixen). The film begins with the famous first sentence from the book: "I had a farm in Africa". The story in the film also draws from other books by Blixen, in particular the stories Shadows on the grass and from autobiographical data of Karen Blixen. The film won seven Oscars, including Best Director and Best Picture. The film received many more awards and received, among other things three Bafta Film Awards and three Golden Globes.
